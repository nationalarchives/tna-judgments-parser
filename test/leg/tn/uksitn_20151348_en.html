Annex 1

TRANSPOSITION NOTE FOR COUNCIL DIRECTIVE 2001/110/EC RELATING TO HONEY (AS AMENDED)

(DIRECTIVE TO DOMESTIC REGULATIONS)

This Note shows how the requirements of Council Directive 2001/110/EC relating to honey (“the Honey Directive”), as last amended by Directive 2014/63/EU of the European Parliament and the Council, have been implemented in England.

As well as the provisions of the Honey Directive, Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 of the European Parliament and of the Council on the provision of food information to consumers applies to the products regulated by the Honey Directive. The name of food provisions in Article 17 of Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 will apply. The names of products prescribed by the Honey Directive are legal names for the purposes of Article 17(1) of the Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011. The requirements relating to the availability, placement and presentation of mandatory particulars will apply to the particulars that are mandatory under the Honey Directive. Article 8 of Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 contains provisions regulating the information that businesses must provide when selling food to other businesses. This includes, for example, an obligation to provide sufficient information to those businesses to enable mandatory food information to be provided to the final consumer and mass caterers when food is eventually sold to them. Enforcement provisions relating to the provisions of Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 are contained in the Food Information Regulations 2014 (S.I. 2014/1855).

Approach of the Regulations

The Honey Directive was originally implemented in England by the Honey (England) Regulations 2003 (S.I. 2003/2243) (“the 2003 Regulations”). However, the 2003 Regulations have been amended by the Honey (Amendment) (England) Regulations 2005 (S.I. 2005/1920) and the Food Information Regulations 2014. Now that the Honey Directive has been amended (by Directive 2014/63/EU), it has decided to replace the 2003 Regulations with updated domestic Regulations which implement the Honey Directive, as amended by Directive 2014/63/EU.

The requirements of the Honey Directive, as amended, will be implemented by the Honey (England) Regulations 2015 (“the 2015 Regulations”) as follows:

Limited scope

The opening words of Article 2 of the Honey Directive state that Article 2000/13/EC is to apply to the Annex I products. The 2000/13/EC reference must, as explained above, be construed as a 1169/2011 reference now.

As Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 only applies (at least for the most part) to food intended for supply to a final consumer or mass caterer, then (because of the opening words of Article 2 of the Honey Directive) so should our domestic legislation implementing the Honey Directive. This implemented by regulation 4 and this approach to scope mirrors the approach taken in the Honey (England) Regulations 2003.

Not all of the provisions of the Honey Directive are only intended to apply the food intended for supply to a final consumer or mass caterers. Some provisions are of wider scope. This is why the scope provision in regulation 4(1) of the 2015 Regulations is limited to the provisions of the 2015 Regulations listed in (a) to (c) of that paragraph.

Directive 2000/13/EC was repealed by Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011.

By virtue of Article 53(2) of Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011, references to Directive 2000/13/EC in the Honey Directive are now treated as references to Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011.

The provisions in Article 12 Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 relating to the availability and placement of mandatory food information, and the provisions of Article 13(1) of that Regulation relating to the presentation of mandatory food information, apply, by virtue of Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011, to the presentation of mandatory food information.

‘Mandatory food information’ is defined as meaning ‘the particulars that are required to be provided to the final consumer by Union provisions’. These will include the country of origin indication that is required to be provided under point 4(a) of Article 2 of the Honey Directive.

Enforcement provisions enabling compliance with Articles 12 and 13(1) of Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 to be enforced in England are contained in the Food Information Regulations 2014.

This is the position for all mandatory food information.

However, there is an additional element in relation to the country of origin information required by the Honey Directive. Not only must that country of origin information be treated as ‘mandatory food information’, it must be treated as if it was mandatory food information required by Article 9 of Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 (in succession to Article 3 of Directive 2000/13/EC). This makes a difference when it comes to the minimum font size that must be used for that country of origin information.

The 2015 Regulations amend the Food Information Regulations 2014 so that the country of origin indication must is treated in the same way as mandatory particulars required by Article 9(1). This means that the minimum font size provisions in Article 13(2) and (3) of Regulation (EU) No 1169/2011 apply to them. The consequential amendments made by regulation 21 of the 2015 Regulations as read with paragraphs 1 and 2 of Schedule 3 to the Regulations, have this effect.
